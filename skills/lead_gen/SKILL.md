# Lead Generation Specialist Skills

## ICP Development Framework
1. **Demographics**: Company size, industry, role, location
2. **Psychographics**: Goals, frustrations, values, buying behavior
3. **Behaviors**: Tools they use, communities they're in, content they consume
4. **Triggers**: Events that make them search for a solution

## Lead Sourcing Channels
- **Reddit**: Find people actively complaining about the problem
- **Twitter/X**: Search for pain point keywords, follow relevant hashtags
- **LinkedIn**: Industry groups, job title searches, Sales Navigator
- **IndieHackers**: Founders in the target market
- **Directories**: Industry directories, review sites (G2, Capterra)
- **Communities**: Slack groups, Discord servers, Facebook groups
- **SEO**: Create content targeting problem-aware search queries

## Outreach Templates
- **Cold Email**: Pain-problem-solution framework (3 sentences max)
- **Reddit Comment**: Helpful answer + subtle mention
- **Twitter DM**: Engage first, pitch second
- **LinkedIn Connection**: Shared context + value-first message
- **Community Post**: Share genuine insights, build trust over time

## Lead Scoring Criteria
- **Intent Signals**: Actively searching for solutions (High)
- **Fit Score**: Matches ICP demographics and behaviors (Medium-High)
- **Engagement**: Responded to outreach or content (Medium)
- **Budget**: Has the ability to pay (Medium)
- **Timeline**: Needs a solution now vs. later (High for "now")

## Quick Wins (First 50 Customers)
1. Post in 5-10 relevant subreddits with genuine value
2. Reply to every tweet about the pain point
3. Launch on ProductHunt with an optimized page
4. Write a comparison blog post targeting "[competitor] alternative"
5. Engage daily in 3-5 communities where ICPs hang out
